The Ritual Column

Build routines that actually hold.

A habit tracker shouldn't make you feel bad on the day you skip. Zain is built around streaks you earn and checklists that support you when the day gets complicated.

  • Daily, weekly, and checklist habits — with their own rhythm.
  • Streak calendars that celebrate, not shame.
  • Challenges with friends — one small thing, done together.
  • Gentle reminders — never nagging, never noisy.
The Library Desk

Read what matters. Remember what you read.

A shelf for every book you're in the middle of. A place for the passages and thoughts that would otherwise slip away. Book clubs with people you actually want to read alongside.

  • Track currently reading, finished, and to-read.
  • Highlights and notes, kept with each book.
  • Reading goals without the pressure of a leaderboard.
  • Private book clubs — small, quiet, real.
The Journal

A softer place than a blank page.

Gratitude on the mornings you need it. Reflection when the day asks more of you. Lists, letters, and the small observations that become a life, re-read later.

  • Six entry types, each with its own mood and palette.
  • Offline-first — write on a plane, on a walk, anywhere.
  • Searchable, private, yours.
  • Optional prompts, never required.
The Gazette

A feed that roots for you.

A community where the front page is book clubs, habit challenges, and small wins. No public follower counts. No outrage. No infinite scroll optimised to hurt you.

  • Posts that celebrate streaks, books, and reflections.
  • Book clubs with shared notes and discussions.
  • Habit challenges with your closest people.
  • A feed you can close, and not miss.
The Broadsheet

The quiet dossier of a body in motion.

Steps, walks, workouts, and personal records — kept the way a morning sports broadsheet would keep them. Clean newsprint. Earned gold for the records that matter.

  • Apple Health and Garmin sync, set-and-forget.
  • Personal records — celebrated in trophy gold.
  • Daily, weekly, and monthly summaries.
  • Fitness challenges with friends, not strangers.
A day in Zain

How the day holds together.

A companion is only useful if it fits the rhythm of your day. Here's how ours tends to.

  1. 06:12

    The morning ritual

    Three habits you care about, quietly checked. A gratitude note if the day calls for one.

  2. 10:40

    A chapter before work

    Ten pages. A highlight. A passing thought, kept with the book.

  3. 17:30

    A walk, logged

    Apple Health hands it over. You don't need to look — the Broadsheet keeps the record.

  4. 20:05

    A note to yourself

    A short reflection. A gratitude. A list of things the day gave you.

  5. 22:18

    A quiet close

    Three habits done. A book page ahead. Tomorrow already a little gentler.
